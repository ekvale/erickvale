What We Do

What you can build on this stack

The HTAC narrative and demo pipeline on this domain are aligned with OHDSI’s OMOP CDM and federated patterns. Resources under the HTAC app (including OHDSI links and population-health references) inform the framing below.

HTAC

Health Trends Across Communities

Privacy-preserving record linkage, enrichment, stratification, and suppression so prevalence and trend estimates can move between sites without exposing identifiable microdata.

  • Step-by-step pipeline pages with field-level notes
  • Governance-oriented language for DUAs and oversight
  • Sandbox-style illustration, not an official reporting system

OMOP CDM

Common data model & cohort logic

Clinical data stay with contributors in OMOP; cohort definitions and federated execution patterns keep analytics portable across jurisdictions and vendors.

  • Shared vocabulary and tables for consistent measures
  • OHDSI tooling and community practices as the backbone
  • Interoperability between operational systems and published aggregates

See also: ohdsi.org and the HTAC about page for how OMOP fits the HTAC workflow.

HDU

Health data utility

Turning standardized models and governed aggregates into usable products: documented HTTPS APIs, reproducible tables for leadership, and handoff materials so technical and program teams share one picture.

  • Documented read-only HTTPS interface for integration testing
  • Clear separation between contributor-held data and published outputs
  • Room to pair analytics with narrative documentation for policy audiences

Implementation

End-to-end pipeline design, demonstration environments, and handoff materials so your technical and governance leads share the same picture.

Governance

DUA alignment, stratification and suppression policy, Tribal and FQHC inclusion planning, and documentation for oversight bodies.

Interoperability

OMOP CDM, federated execution patterns, and service boundaries between operational systems and published analytics products.
