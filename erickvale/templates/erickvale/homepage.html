What you can build on this stack

The HTAC narrative and demo pipeline on this domain are aligned with OHDSI’s OMOP CDM and federated patterns. Resources under the HTAC app (including OHDSI links and population-health references) inform the framing below.

HTAC

Health Trends Across Communities

Privacy-preserving record linkage, enrichment, stratification, and suppression so prevalence and trend estimates can move between sites without exposing identifiable microdata.

  • Step-by-step pipeline pages with field-level notes
  • Governance-oriented language for DUAs and oversight
  • Sandbox-style illustration, not an official reporting system

OMOP CDM

Common data model & cohort logic

Clinical data stay with contributors in OMOP; cohort definitions and federated execution patterns keep analytics portable across jurisdictions and vendors.

  • Shared vocabulary and tables for consistent measures
  • OHDSI tooling and community practices as the backbone
  • Interoperability between operational systems and published aggregates

See also: ohdsi.org and the HTAC about page for how OMOP fits the HTAC workflow.

HDU

Health data utility

Turning standardized models and governed aggregates into documented, accessible outputs: HTTPS APIs, reproducible tables for leadership, and handoff materials so technical and program teams share one picture.

  • Documented read-only HTTPS interface for integration testing
  • Clear separation between contributor-held data and published outputs
  • Structured to pair analytic outputs with narrative documentation for oversight bodies and program decision-makers

Implementation

End-to-end pipeline design and deployment — from OMOP CDM configuration and vocabulary mapping at contributing sites through privacy-preserving record linkage, federated query execution, and aggregate results published to governed outputs. Includes handoff documentation so technical and oversight staff are working from a consistent implementation record.

Governance

Data use agreement alignment, scientific review pathway design, suppression and stratification policy, Tribal and FQHC inclusion planning, and documentation structured for oversight bodies and institutional review. Governance is a design input, not a compliance afterthought.

Interoperability

OMOP CDM as the shared data standard across contributing sites, with OHDSI tooling for cohort definition, quality assessment, and federated execution. Service boundaries between contributor-held data, internal aggregate tables, and externally published outputs are explicit in the architecture.
